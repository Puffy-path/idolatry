# Idolatry, False Gods, and Forbidden Practices in the Book of 1 Samuel (LSV and NRSV)

This document presents a comprehensive list of verses from the **Book of 1 Samuel** that reference **idolatry**, **false gods**, **praying to angels**, or **other forbidden religious practices**. Each verse is shown in both the **Literal Standard Version (LSV)** and the **New Revised Standard Version (NRSV)** for easy comparison.

> ✅ *If a verse is only present or relevant in one translation, both versions are still listed. If the corresponding verse in the other translation doesn’t mention the theme, it is still included for context.*

---

## 1 Samuel 5:2-4  
**LSV:**  
"And the Philistines take the Ark of God and bring it into the house of Dagon, and set it near Dagon. And the people of Ashdod rise early on the next day, and behold, Dagon has fallen on its face to the earth before the Ark of YHWH; and they take Dagon and turn him back to his place. And they rise early in the morning on the next day, and behold, Dagon has fallen on its face to the earth before the Ark of YHWH, and the head of Dagon and the two palms of his hands cut off at the threshold, only the torso of Dagon has been left to him."

**NRSV:**  
"The Philistines took the ark of God and brought it into the house of Dagon and placed it beside Dagon. When the people of Ashdod rose early the next day, there was Dagon, fallen on his face to the ground before the ark of the LORD. So they took Dagon and put him back in his place. But when they rose early on the next morning, Dagon had fallen on his face to the ground before the ark of the LORD, and the head of Dagon and both the hands of Dagon were lying cut off upon the threshold; only the trunk of Dagon was left to him."

## 1 Samuel 7:3-4  
**LSV:**  
"And Samuel speaks to all the house of Israel, saying, 'If you are returning to YHWH with all your heart, turn aside the gods of the stranger from your midst, and the Ashtaroth, and prepare your heart to YHWH, and serve Him only; and He delivers you out of the hand of the Philistines.' And the sons of Israel turn aside the Ba‘alim and the Ashtaroth and serve YHWH alone."

**NRSV:**  
"Then Samuel said to all the house of Israel, 'If you are returning to the LORD with all your heart, then put away the foreign gods and the Astartes from among you. Direct your heart to the LORD, and serve him only, and he will deliver you out of the hand of the Philistines.' So the Israelites put away the Baals and the Astartes, and they served the LORD only."

---

## 1 Samuel 8:19-20  
**LSV:**  
"And the people refuse to listen to the voice of Samuel, and say, 'No, but a king is over us, and we have been, we also, like all the nations, and our king has judged us, and gone out before us, and fought our battles.'"

**NRSV:**  
"But the people refused to listen to the voice of Samuel; they said, 'No! But we are determined to have a king over us, so that we also may be like other nations, and that our king may govern us and go out before us and fight our battles.'"

## 1 Samuel 12:21  
**LSV:**  
"And you do not turn aside after the emptiness which does not profit nor deliver, for they [are] emptiness."

**NRSV:**  
"And do not turn aside after useless things that cannot profit or save, for they are useless."

## 1 Samuel 15:23  
**LSV:**  
"For rebellion [is as] the sin of divination, and stubbornness [is as] iniquity and teraphim; because you have rejected the word of YHWH, He also rejects you from [being] king."

**NRSV:**  
"For rebellion is no less a sin than divination, and stubbornness is like iniquity and idolatry. Because you have rejected the word of the LORD, he has also rejected you from being king."

## 1 Samuel 28:7-8  
**LSV:**  
"And Saul says to his servants, 'Seek for me a woman possessing a familiar spirit, and I go to her, and inquire of her'; and his servants say to him, 'Behold, a woman possessing a familiar spirit [is] in En-dor.' And Saul disguises himself and puts on other garments, and goes—he and two of the men with him—and they come to the woman by night, and he says, 'Please divine for me with the familiar spirit, and bring up for me him whom I say to you.'"

**NRSV:**  
"Then Saul said to his servants, 'Seek out for me a woman who is a medium, so that I may go to her and inquire of her.' His servants said to him, 'There is a medium at Endor.' So Saul disguised himself and put on other clothes and went there, he and two men with him. They came to the woman by night, and he said, 'Consult a spirit for me, and bring up for me the one whom I name to you.'"

## Notes  
- **Dagon’s fall (1 Samuel 5):** Highlights the impotence of foreign gods before YHWH.
- **Removal of foreign gods (1 Samuel 7):** Samuel calls Israel to wholehearted devotion to YHWH.
- **Desire for a king (1 Samuel 8):** Reflects Israel’s temptation to conform to surrounding nations.
- **Emptiness and uselessness (1 Samuel 12:21):** Refers to the futility of idolatry.
- **Divination and teraphim (1 Samuel 15):** Equates rebellion and stubbornness with forbidden practices.
- **Saul and the medium (1 Samuel 28):** Illustrates the dangers of seeking spiritual guidance outside of God.
